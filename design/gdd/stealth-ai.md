## Overview

Stealth AI is simultaneously the **graduated-suspicion engine** that makes *The Paris Affair*'s stealth feel theatrical (Pillar 3) AND the **NPC guards** patrolling the Eiffel Tower whom the player outwits. The engine consumes Player Character's published perception surface â€” `get_noise_level()`, `get_noise_event()`, `get_silhouette_height()`, `global_transform.origin` (F.4 + F.8) â€” runs its own dual-channel perception (vision cones + hearing polls) with occlusion and distance modifiers, and drives each guard through a four-state alert lattice: **Unaware â†’ Suspicious â†’ Searching â†’ Combat**. All three non-combat transitions are fully reversible â€” a guard who was briefly suspicious but lost the cue returns to Unaware (Pillar 3: *"Stealth is Theatre, Not Punishment"*).

Guards are `CharacterBody3D` NPCs with `NavigationAgent3D` patrol routes, a vision `Area3D` approximating their FOV cone, and a hearing poller running at 10 Hz on the player's noise surface. They publish four signals through the `Events` bus per ADR-0002: `alert_state_changed`, `actor_became_alerted`, `actor_lost_target`, `takedown_performed`. Audio subscribes for music ducking and stingers; Mission Scripting subscribes for objective triggers; Civilian AI subscribes for secondary-observer behaviour; Dialogue & Subtitles subscribes for contextual guard banter.

This is the **gating technical-risk system** per the game concept (line 223): graduated suspicion is the longest implementation pole. Perception-balance tuning happens against PC's published noise values (Walk 5 m / Sprint 12 m / Crouch 3 m per F.4; hard-landing spikes 8â€“16 m per F.3 scaled), locked to ship-value 1.0 multiplier. Takedowns at MVP: **melee non-lethal** (chloroform-style knockout from behind) + **silenced pistol** (lethal) â€” Inventory & Gadgets owns the verb implementations; Stealth AI owns the target-side effect (guard drops, state transitions to `Dead`, patrol route vacated).

**This GDD defines**: the 4-state alert lattice, perception math (sight + sound), patrol + investigate + flee behaviors, takedown target-side effects, signal contract. **This GDD does NOT define**: takedown weapon specs (Inventory & Gadgets), guard dialogue (Dialogue & Subtitles), patrol route authoring (Mission & Level Scripting), guard models/outline tiers (Art Bible + ADR-0001), save format (Save/Load consumes state via ADR-0003).

## Player Fantasy

**"Theatre, Not Punishment."** Guards in *The Paris Affair* are the **co-stars** of the player's stealth comedy, not obstacles to be optimized around. When a guard walks past Eve's hiding spot, the player feels the shape of a *scene*: the guard's footfalls crescendo, the player holds their breath, the guard arrives, glances, shrugs, walks on. The fantasy is **outwitting a competent opponent who takes themselves seriously** â€” not evading a video-game robot. The comedy lives in the space between the guard's self-importance and the absurdity of the situation.

**The graduated-suspicion model is the fantasy** â€” not a mechanic supporting one. Each state reads as a recognisable human reaction:

- **Unaware** â€” The guard is doing their job. Patrolling, smoking, muttering about dinner. Period-authentic dialogue. You can walk by (quietly), wait for them to turn the corner, or slip past.
- **Suspicious** â€” *"What was that?"* The guard has heard or glimpsed something; they haven't localised it. They stop. They look around. They call out ("أ‡a va?" or equivalent per future localisation). If nothing confirms the cue within ~4 seconds, they shrug and return to Unaware. This state is **diegetically legible** â€” the player hears the stop, hears the breath, sees the head turn.
- **Searching** â€” *"Something is definitely off."* The guard leaves their patrol and walks toward the last known position. They sweep the area, peer behind crates, open closets. They don't see Eve yet, but they're committed. If they find her, Combat. If they don't, Suspicious again (briefly), then Unaware. **This is the NOLF1 comedy moment** â€” the player hides, the guard investigates right past them, mutters *"Hmph, nothing"*, and goes back to patrol. The player feels smart; the guard feels stupid; nobody dies.
- **Combat** â€” *"Got you."* Guard has positively identified Eve. Engages â€” draws weapon, moves to cover, fires. This is the fail-forward state: the game continues, escape routes are open, other guards may be alerted via `actor_became_alerted` propagation, but the player isn't dead and isn't kicked to a load screen. **Combat is reversible only via distance + time** (the guard loses sight for > 8 seconds AND Eve is out of vision cone â†’ back to Searching).

**References:** Emma Peel and Steed cat-burgling a villain's estate while the guards bumble around them (*The Avengers*). Cate Archer evading patrols in NOLF1's intro museum level. Garrett in *Thief* crouched in a shadow while the guard sweeps the room. Guards in *Hitman: Blood Money* with the extra comedy beat on false alarms.

**Pillars served:**
- **Pillar 3** (Stealth is Theatre, Not Punishment) â€” this system IS Pillar 3 kinesthetically. Reversibility is the load-bearing design choice. Detection opens a scene, it doesn't close the game.
- **Pillar 1** (Comedy Without Punchlines) â€” guard banter during Unaware + Suspicious is the primary comedy vector. Eve doesn't quip; the guards do, obliviously.
- **Pillar 5** (Period Authenticity) â€” no modern UX: no alert icon above the guard's head, no "!" meter, no minimap, no last-known-position marker on the HUD. Alert state is signaled through **audio + body language only** (Game Concept: *"Guard alert state shown via subtle audio cues + body language, not UI HUD bars"*).
- **Pillar 4** (Iconic Locations as Co-Stars) â€” *supporting* (added 2026-04-28 per `/review-all-gdds` 2026-04-28 finding 2f / GD-B1 carryforward closure). SAI's vertical line-of-sight cones, perception-reachability checks, and patrol-route geometry are **architecture-coupled** to the Eiffel Tower's specific verticality: guards on the Plaza must look up at the scaffolds; guards on the Restaurant level must check the spiral staircase; guards on the Upper Structure patrol the ironwork lattice with constrained sight-lines through the diagonal beams. The same SAI configuration on a flat level would feel mechanically equivalent but tonally generic â€” the Tower's geometry shapes which patrols are dangerous and which are bypassable. Without Tower architecture, SAI is correct but unspecific; with it, every encounter feels Eiffel-Tower-shaped. Pillar 4 supporting role is a **content-authoring rule** more than a code mechanism (level designers must place patrol routes that exploit the specific verticality), but SAI's perception model is the substrate that makes the geometry tactically meaningful.

**Design test:** *If the player ever says "that guard is bugged" instead of "that guard is funny/dangerous/suspicious" â€” we've failed the fantasy.* Guards must read as characters with lives, not as state machines with bugs.

**Kinesthetic specifics:**
- Footstep audio from guards is the primary "incoming guard" cue at distance (authored as guard-side audio parallel to PC's FootstepComponent â€” not in this GDD, but Stealth AI emits position for 3D audio).
- Alert state transitions are announced via **music shifts** (Audio GDD owns this: `alert_state_changed` â†’ music stinger or bed change).
- Each guard has a **unique voice line pool** per section; repetition kills the comedy.
- When a guard enters Searching mode, they have a **characteristic investigate walk** (slower than patrol, slightly hunched, weapon drawn but not raised) â€” readable silhouette at distance.

## Detailed Design

### Core Rules

**Guard node architecture.** Each guard is a `CharacterBody3D` named after its type (e.g., `GuardPatrol`, `GuardStationary`) with the following children:

- `NavigationAgent3D` â€” pathfinding. Navigation mesh is authored per-section by Mission & Level Scripting (outside this GDD's scope).
- `VisionCone: Area3D` â€” Godot 4.6 has no `ConeShape3D`, so FOV is approximated by a **`SphereShape3D`** of radius `VISION_MAX_RANGE_M` with angle filtering performed in the `body_entered(body: Node3D)` callback via dot-product. **Forward axis:** Godot 3D uses `-basis.z` as the forward vector; the guard's world-forward is `-guard.global_transform.basis.z` (NOT `guard.basis * Vector3.FORWARD`, which is +X and points to the guard's right). **Downward tilt:** the reference forward vector is rotated about the guard's local X axis by `-VISION_CONE_DOWNWARD_ANGLE_DEG` degrees before the dot-product check: `eye_forward = (-guard.global_transform.basis.z).rotated(guard.global_transform.basis.x, -deg_to_rad(VISION_CONE_DOWNWARD_ANGLE_DEG))`. Accept iff `eye_forward.dot((body.global_position - guard.eye_position).normalized()) >= cos(deg_to_rad(VISION_FOV_DEG / 2.0))`. **Zero-distance edge**: if `(body.global_position - guard.eye_position).length() < 0.1` (Eve inside the eye point), the dot-product would be computed against a zero vector â€” short-circuit and ACCEPT unconditionally (Eve touching the guard's face is tautologically seen). Area3D fields: `layer = 0`, `mask = PhysicsLayers.MASK_PLAYER | PhysicsLayers.MASK_AI` (player-body perception + dead-guard-body perception for SAW_BODY; live guards and civilians on LAYER_AI are filtered out by the group check below). **Occluders MUST NOT be in the Area3D mask** â€” that would cause `body_entered` to fire on every piece of world geometry. Occluder detection happens exclusively via the F.1 LOS raycast against `MASK_AI_VISION_OCCLUDERS`. Perception flow: `body_entered(body: Node3D)` â†’ reject unless `body.is_in_group("player")` OR `body.is_in_group("dead_guard")` (live guards on LAYER_AI that are in `alive_guard` group are rejected here â†’ NO false-positive AI-vs-AI sight; civilians if/when added must NOT be in either group) â†’ belt-and-braces: also reject unless `body is PlayerCharacter` or `body is GuardBase` (typed class check guards against group-tag misuse during scene authoring) â†’ dot-product angle filter (with zero-distance short-circuit above) â†’ if accepted, begin feeding the sight accumulator per F.1 (which additionally gates each physics frame on its own LOS raycast).
- `HearingPoller: Node` â€” non-physics child that runs `_physics_process` at 10 Hz via internal tick counter, calling `player.get_noise_level()` + `player.get_noise_event()` and accumulating sound-suspicion. **Poll-phase stagger**: the tick counter initial value is `get_instance_id() % 6` (so 12 co-spawned guards are spread across the 6-frame period instead of all firing on the same physics frame). Stagger prevents a 12-guard burst every 100 ms; it does not change aggregate 10 Hz per-guard rate.
- `Perception: Node` â€” suspicion accumulators (one per channel) + decay timers + last-known-position storage.
- `Weapon: MeshInstance3D` â€” holstered at Unaware/Suspicious; drawn at Searching; raised at Combat. Actual weapon mechanics (fire rate, damage) owned by Combat & Damage GDD.
- `DialogueAnchor: Node3D` â€” position for Dialogue & Subtitles to attach voice-line playback.
- `OutlineTier: ...` â€” guards are tier MEDIUM per ADR-0001. **Implementation constraint**: the outline is applied via `material_overlay` on the guard's `MeshInstance3D` child (not `material_override`, which would replace the base material and break albedo). Guard scene authoring must not set `material_override` on the mesh.

Guards sit on `PhysicsLayers.LAYER_AI` per ADR-0006. `CharacterBody3D._ready()` sets `layer = MASK_AI`, `mask = MASK_WORLD | MASK_PLAYER` (guards cannot walk through Eve; collide with world geometry).

**Alert state ownership.** Each guard holds a single `current_alert_state: StealthAI.AlertState`. The enum lives on `StealthAI` per ADR-0002 Implementation Guideline 2 â€” NOT on `Events.gd`:

```gdscript
# res://src/gameplay/stealth/stealth_ai.gd
class_name StealthAI
extends Node

enum AlertState { UNAWARE, SUSPICIOUS, SEARCHING, COMBAT, UNCONSCIOUS, DEAD }
enum AlertCause { HEARD_NOISE, SAW_PLAYER, SAW_BODY, HEARD_GUNFIRE, ALERTED_BY_OTHER, SCRIPTED, CURIOSITY_BAIT }
enum Severity { MINOR, MAJOR }
```

Neither `DEAD` nor `UNCONSCIOUS` is reachable through perception. Both are terminal-effect states set only by takedown or damage resolution (see Takedown target-side effect below). `DEAD` corresponds to lethal damage (`BULLET`, `MELEE_BLADE`, `FALL_OUT_OF_BOUNDS`) OR `STEALTH_BLADE` takedown (which delegates to Combat as lethal `MELEE_BLADE` per Combat CR-15). `UNCONSCIOUS` corresponds to non-lethal damage reaching 0 HP (`DART_TRANQUILISER`, `MELEE_FIST` â€” per Combat CR-16) OR `MELEE_NONLETHAL` chloroform takedown. The 4 graduated-suspicion states (`UNAWARE`, `SUSPICIOUS`, `SEARCHING`, `COMBAT`) are all reachable through perception. `UNCONSCIOUS â†’ DEAD` is the single Transitional edge between terminal-effect states: further lethal damage on an UNCONSCIOUS guard transitions to DEAD (Combat CR-16 Transitional model); further non-lethal damage (DART / FIST) on UNCONSCIOUS is an idempotent no-op.

**Severity rule** (applied to every emission of `alert_state_changed`, `actor_became_alerted`, `actor_lost_target`):

```gdscript
func _compute_severity(new_state: AlertState, cause: AlertCause) -> Severity:
    if cause == AlertCause.ALERTED_BY_OTHER:
        return Severity.MINOR  # Propagation bump never produces MAJOR
    if new_state == AlertState.SEARCHING or new_state == AlertState.COMBAT \
       or new_state == AlertState.DEAD or new_state == AlertState.UNCONSCIOUS:
        return Severity.MAJOR
    return Severity.MINOR
```

Rationale for DEAD / UNCONSCIOUS â†’ MAJOR: a guard's removal from play is a state-transition consumers must treat as high-salience (Mission Scripting triggers, Audio clean-up, Dialogue barks). Both terminal-effect states fire `actor_lost_target` on the damage-resolution path as MAJOR because the event surfaces a decisive gameplay outcome (guard removed from play). The Transitional UNCONSCIOUS â†’ DEAD edge also emits MAJOR (death is decisive even from an already-removed-from-play starting state). Takedown emissions fire `takedown_performed` (separate signal, no severity field â€” always treated as MAJOR-equivalent by Audio's takedown SFX routing per `takedown_type`).

Consumer contract: **Audio plays the brass-punch stinger ONLY on `severity == MAJOR`.** MINOR transitions cause subtle music-bed shifts but no dramatic stingers. This preserves Pillar 1 comedy (a guard's casual "أ‡a va?" investigation remains a quiet scene, not a siren) and Pillar 3 reversibility (propagation waves do not cascade dramatic audio).

**`CURIOSITY_BAIT` AlertCause** â€” reserved for player-initiated comedy triggers (e.g., knocking over a vase, whistling). Creates a SUSPICIOUS-only investigation using the ordinary state-machine semantics: accumulator fills per F.2b (bait-emitter supplies a NoiseEvent), decays per F.3, de-escalates per the SUSPICIOUS â†’ UNAWARE timeout. **No state-machine dwell floor.** The comedy-beat timing guarantee (mutter vocal plays to completion regardless of state) is OWNED BY DIALOGUE & SUBTITLES via vocal scheduling: Dialogue schedules the mutter vocal non-preemptively â€” once started it plays through to end, even if the guard has already de-escalated to UNAWARE by then (the player hears the guard finish the mutter mid-walk-off, which *is* the comedy). Emitters (Inventory & Gadgets forward-gated verbs) set `cause = CURIOSITY_BAIT` explicitly; propagation from `CURIOSITY_BAIT` does NOT fire (it is a self-contained comedy beat, not an alarm; see F.4 suppression list). **Design rationale for moving the dwell out of the state machine**: a dwell floor in the state machine creates exploitability (player freezes guards via vase-throw) and traps (player cannot de-escalate during their own bait's dwell window). Vocal-scheduling guarantees deliver the comedy without interfering with perceptual state. Reference: game-designer B-1 adversarial finding, 2026-04-21 re-review.

**Dual-channel perception model.** Each guard runs two independent suspicion accumulators, each with its own threshold for triggering state escalation:

- **Sight suspicion** (`_sight_accumulator: float`, 0.0â€“1.0) â€” filled when Eve is inside the vision cone AND the LOS raycast succeeds AND her silhouette clears the relevant height band. Fill rate depends on range + silhouette height + Eve's movement state. Decays when LOS breaks.
- **Sound suspicion** (`_sound_accumulator: float`, 0.0â€“1.0) â€” filled when `player.get_noise_level()` exceeds a distance-occlusion-adjusted audibility threshold OR when `player.get_noise_event()` returns a non-null spike whose effective radius reaches the guard after propagation. Fill rate depends on how far noise exceeds threshold + event type. Decays over time when no audible source.

**Channel independence rationale**: NOLF1-style legibility. A player hears a guard react to *what they did* â€” "I heard something" vs "I saw something" produces different AlertCause values, different vocal callout pools, and (Dialogue GDD forward dep) different banter. Keeping the accumulators independent preserves decay semantics and diagnosis, while escalation still responds to combined cues via the score below.

**Channel combination score.** Escalation is gated on a **weighted combined score**:

```
combined = max(_sight, _sound) + 0.5 أ— min(_sight, _sound)
```

Rationale: a guard who partially hears AND partially sees Eve (e.g., `_sight = 0.25`, `_sound = 0.25`) should notice something (combined = 0.375, crosses `T_SUSPICIOUS = 0.3`), rather than ignore both because neither channel alone crossed threshold. The dominant channel carries full weight; the secondary channel contributes half. This preserves the NOLF1 sight-dominance feel (sight is usually the escalating channel) while closing the combinatorial blindspot. Both accumulators remain independently decayed per F.3; `combined` is a derived value, not a stored accumulator.

**State escalation rule.** When `combined` reaches a threshold, the guard transitions up one state:

- `combined >= T_SUSPICIOUS` â†’ transition to SUSPICIOUS (if currently UNAWARE)
- `combined >= T_SEARCHING` â†’ transition to SEARCHING (if currently UNAWARE or SUSPICIOUS)
- `combined >= T_COMBAT` â†’ transition to COMBAT (if currently any non-combat, non-dead state)

The AlertCause of the escalation is tie-broken by whichever accumulator contributed the larger term: if `_sight >= _sound` the cause derives from the last sight stimulus (`SAW_PLAYER` or `SAW_BODY`); otherwise from the last sound stimulus (`HEARD_NOISE`, `HEARD_GUNFIRE`, `ALERTED_BY_OTHER`). If the guard was bumped via `PROPAGATION_BUMP` this physics frame, cause is always `ALERTED_BY_OTHER`.

**State de-escalation rule.** When `combined` falls below a *lower* de-escalate threshold AND a cooldown timer expires, the guard transitions down one state:

- SEARCHING â†’ SUSPICIOUS after `SEARCH_TIMEOUT_SEC` if `combined < T_DECAY_SEARCHING` and no new stimulus
- SUSPICIOUS â†’ UNAWARE after `SUSPICION_TIMEOUT_SEC` if `combined < T_DECAY_UNAWARE`
- COMBAT â†’ SEARCHING after `COMBAT_LOST_TARGET_SEC` of no sight AND no direct hit

**No state-machine dwell floor** (including for `CURIOSITY_BAIT`): the state machine is purely perceptual and uses only the `SUSPICION_TIMEOUT_SEC` floor. Comedy-vocal timing is owned by Dialogue & Subtitles via non-preemptive vocal scheduling (see CURIOSITY_BAIT note above). This preserves reversibility â€” Pillar 3's load-bearing invariant â€” and prevents the vase-throw exploit / self-trap pattern identified by game-designer B-1 (2026-04-21 adversarial re-review).

**Accumulator decay.** When no stimulus is present, each accumulator decays exponentially:

`accumulator -= DECAY_RATE * delta`

Where `DECAY_RATE` varies per state (faster decay in UNAWARE, slower in COMBAT â€” the guard "remembers" recent alarm longer when hostile). Decay never goes below 0.

**Last-known-position (LKP).** When any accumulator crosses `T_SEARCHING` or higher, the perception module stores the world-space position that produced the stimulus:

- For hearing: `origin` field from the latched `NoiseEvent`, OR `player.global_transform.origin` for continuous-locomotion noise.
- For sight: the position of the first LOS-confirmed cell in the vision cone.

LKP is a single `Vector3` per guard, overwritten by newer stimuli. When the guard enters SEARCHING, `NavigationAgent3D.target_position = LKP` drives them toward it.

**Investigate behavior (SEARCHING).** Guard navigates to LKP. On arrival (within `INVESTIGATE_ARRIVAL_EPSILON_M` of LKP), plays a sweep animation (look left, look right, open a nearby closet/crate if authored). Stops for `INVESTIGATE_SWEEP_SEC`. If stimulus returns during sweep â†’ extends the timer. If no new stimulus â†’ decays both accumulators rapidly and transitions back to SUSPICIOUS â†’ UNAWARE through the normal de-escalate path.

**Combat behavior (COMBAT).** Guard draws weapon if not already drawn. Navigates to cover (uses tactical pathfinding â€” beyond MVP, accepts hand-authored cover nodes). Fires at last-known-sight position (not LKP â€” sight is the confirm-channel for combat). If sight re-confirms, tracks target. If sight lost for > `COMBAT_LOST_TARGET_SEC`, de-escalates.

**COMBAT â†’ UNAWARE recovery pacing spec** (Pillar 3 theatre contract). The full recovery arc is COMBAT â†’ SEARCHING â†’ SUSPICIOUS â†’ UNAWARE, with a theoretical minimum of `COMBAT_LOST_TARGET_SEC (8s) + SEARCH_TIMEOUT_SEC (12s, if SEARCHING proceeds from accumulator reset) + SUSPICION_TIMEOUT_SEC (4s) â‰ˆ 20 s+` total. This long arc risks reading as punishment rather than theatre (game-designer B-2 concern). The design response is that the arc must be **kinesthetically legible as tension-releasing**, not just a timer crawl:
- **t+0 to t+8s (COMBAT, no-sight-no-damage)**: guard continues cover-fire at last-sight position, then gradually stops firing, weapon still raised. Audio: combat music persists (dominant-guard rule). Vocal: agitated shout cadence every 2â€“3 s ("Where did she go?!"). This is the "guard is hunting" beat.
- **t+8s (COMBAT â†’ SEARCHING transition, MAJOR)**: weapon lowers from aim to ready-at-hip; `actor_lost_target(MAJOR)` fires â†’ Audio plays woodwind decay tail (defeated-hunter cue); vocal: frustrated mutter ("Lost her..."). Music begins transition toward `*_searching` bed. This is the "tension releasing" beat.
- **t+8 to t+20s (SEARCHING sweep)**: guard navigates back toward patrol-adjacent area or LKP; vocal cadence shifts to uncertain mutters every 2.5â€“4 s. Music at `*_searching` bed (lower intensity than `*_combat`). Guard is on-edge but no longer hunting.
- **t+20s+ (SEARCHING â†’ SUSPICIOUS, MINOR)**: weapon holsters; vocal "Hmph, nothing." Music transitions toward `*_suspicious`.
- **t+24s+ (SUSPICIOUS â†’ UNAWARE, MINOR)**: vocal "Must be nothing." Music returns to `*_calm`. Guard resumes patrol route.

Ownership: vocal cadence authoring belongs to **Dialogue & Subtitles** (forward-dep); music transitions belong to **Audio GDD** (already covers via `alert_state_changed` severity-gated transitions); Stealth AI's role is firing the state transitions on schedule. If playtest reveals the arc reads as punishment, the fix is vocal/music pacing, not shortening the timers (which would break reversibility determinism).

**Takedown target-side effect.** When Eve presses the dedicated `Takedown` input (Combat CR-3 + CR-10) while SAI's context prompt is live (see `takedown_prompt_active` accessor below), Inventory & Gadgets invokes `receive_takedown(takedown_type: TakedownType, attacker: Node) -> void` on the target guard. `TakedownType` enum is defined on `StealthAI` (owned here because the receiver owns the consequence):

```gdscript
enum TakedownType { MELEE_NONLETHAL, STEALTH_BLADE }
```

**Terminal-state routing by takedown type** (revised OQ-CD-1, 2026-04-22 â€” chloroform fictionally non-lethal, symmetric with Combat CR-16 MELEE_FIST routing):

- **`MELEE_NONLETHAL` (chloroform)** â†’ guard transitions **synchronously** to `AlertState.UNCONSCIOUS`. Chloroform SFX (Audio) + `chloroformed_slump` pose via captured approach vector. No Combat delegation (no damage applied â€” chloroform is a takedown verb, not a damage event).
- **`STEALTH_BLADE` (concealed blade)** â†’ SAI calls `Combat.apply_damage_to_actor(self, blade_takedown_damage=100, attacker, DamageType.MELEE_BLADE)` synchronously (Combat CR-15). Because `MELEE_BLADE` is lethal per `Combat.is_lethal_damage_type()`, the subsequent `receive_damage` path reduces HP to 0 and transitions `AlertState.DEAD` inside the same call stack. Blade-stroke SFX + muffled body-drop (Audio GDD آ§SFX catalog must include this variant) + `dead_slump` pose via captured approach vector.

**Shared target-side cleanup** (applied synchronously before `takedown_performed` emits â€” identical for both takedown types and for both terminal-effect states):

1. **Capture approach vector**: compute `approach_vector = (attacker.global_transform.origin - self.global_transform.origin).with_y(0).normalized()` (2D, XZ-plane, unit). Store on guard for body-drop animator (آ§V.4 Combat: "face AWAY from Eve's approach"). On the damage path (no `attacker` reference passed to `receive_takedown` â€” e.g., lethal gunfight), use `source` from `receive_damage`; if both are null (scripted despawn), use `-forward_axis` as a safe default.
2. **Synchronous state mutation**: `current_alert_state = <terminal>` (UNCONSCIOUS or DEAD) **before** any emissions, so Combat's CR-2 signal ordering + `GuardFireController`'s defensive gate observe the correct state within the same call stack.
3. **Agent stop**: `NavigationAgent3D.target_position = global_position` (`is_navigation_finished()` becomes true on the NEXT physics frame after nav server sync â€” same-frame callers must check `target_position ==` current position for synchronous verification, not `is_navigation_finished()` directly).
4. **Accumulators zero**: both `_sight_accumulator` and `_sound_accumulator` set to 0.
5. **Disable perception**: `VisionCone.monitoring = false` (stops future `body_entered` / `body_exited` emission on this guard's cone) + `HearingPoller.set_physics_process(false)` (stops `_physics_process` on just the `HearingPoller` child node, not the whole guard â€” Godot per-node physics processing is isolated in 4.6). **Corrected 2026-04-22 per godot-specialist B-1 + CD adjudication**: setting `monitoring = false` does NOT synthetically emit `body_exited` for bodies currently inside the Area3D (this was a prior-revision factual claim that was wrong â€” Godot 4.x Area3D only emits exit signals from genuine physics-step overlap resolution). The `body_exited` handler MUST still early-return when `current_alert_state in [AlertState.DEAD, AlertState.UNCONSCIOUS]` as defense against same-frame emission races (e.g., an authentic physics-step body_exited arrives at the same frame as the terminal transition before monitoring has been disabled). **NEW: cross-guard unregistration protocol** (resolves ai-programmer B-1; signature extended 2026-04-24 per ADR-0002 amendment + Inventory Coord item #7). When a guard enters a terminal-effect state, the guard emits the project-level bus signal **`Events.guard_incapacitated(guard: Node, cause: int)`** AFTER cleanup but BEFORE `alert_state_changed`. The `cause` parameter is the `CombatSystemNode.DamageType` int value that drove the terminal transition (e.g., `DART_TRANQUILISER`, `MELEE_FIST`, `MELEE_BLADE`, `BULLET`, `MELEE_PARFUM` added 2026-04-24). The SAI emit-site reads `_last_damage_type` (captured synchronously during `receive_damage`) and passes it through. Every live guard's VisionCone and dead-body tracking data structures subscribe and explicitly remove the incapacitated guard from their internal tracked-bodies sets. Inventory's CR-7 drop-table router ALSO subscribes to distinguish dart-KO (drops 1 dart) / fist-KO (drops nothing) / Parfum-KO (drops nothing, LOCKED invariant). Without this protocol, a dead guard lying in another guard's cone (but whose `monitoring = false` removes it as an active sensor) would cause the observer to accumulate stale SAW_BODY state indefinitely on reload, or miss the body-sighting transition if the body was in-cone at the moment of incapacitation. **Signal signature was landed in ADR-0002 2026-04-24 amendment** (pre-impl gate #2 CLOSED); atomic-commit requirement applies â€” SAI + Inventory + `events.gd` must land in a single PR (partial merge causes GDScript argument-count mismatch at emit-site).
6. **Group unification**: guard is added to group `dead_guard` and removed from group `alive_guard`. The `dead_guard` group name is retained as a **unified "removed-from-play" tag** meaning "body on the floor" â€” UNCONSCIOUS guards join it too so the same SAW_BODY 2أ—-fill perception rule applies uniformly (a sleeping guard is just as suspicious to other guards as a corpse). Downstream systems that care about the DEAD vs UNCONSCIOUS distinction read `current_alert_state` directly (save format, outline tier, `takedown_type` routing, etc.).
7. **Pose**: apply `chloroformed_slump` (UNCONSCIOUS) or `dead_slump` (DEAD) via the captured approach vector so the guard's face ends AWAY from Eve's incoming direction (آ§V.4 Combat GDD).
8. **Signals** (in this order, all within the same call stack): `alert_state_changed(self, prev_state, terminal_state, MAJOR)` first, then `takedown_performed(self, attacker, takedown_type)` (takedown path) â€” Audio routes the takedown SFX by `takedown_type` (MELEE_NONLETHAL â†’ chloroform, STEALTH_BLADE â†’ blade-stroke + muffled thud per Audio GDD آ§SFX catalog). On the damage path (no takedown), emit `actor_lost_target(self, MAJOR)` instead of `takedown_performed`; the lethal-gunfight path additionally sees Combat emit `enemy_killed(self, source)` in the same call stack per CR-2.

**Takedown eligibility gate** (attack-side). The dedicated `Takedown` input (Combat CR-3 + CR-10) consults SAI's `guard.takedown_prompt_active(eve) -> bool` accessor on every idle tick; input presses while the prompt is inactive drop silently (no animation, no audio, no SAI call). If a call still reaches `receive_takedown` (e.g., race between player input and state transition): SEARCHING / COMBAT guards **resist** â€” the attempt fails and triggers `actor_became_alerted(guard, SAW_PLAYER, eve.position, MAJOR)` with no state change. UNCONSCIOUS / DEAD guards drop the call silently (idempotent no-op; see آ§E edge cases for "re-takedown on UNCONSCIOUS").

**Dead-body visibility.** A dead guard is an observable stimulus for OTHER guards. Dead guards are in group `dead_guard` (live guards are in group `alive_guard`). The VisionCone's `body_entered` filter accepts both groups (see Core Rules). Any live guard whose vision cone encompasses a dead body with LOS confirms **sight suspicion at 2أ—-fill-rate** (seeing a corpse is faster-escalating than seeing a moving Eve). The alert cause is `SAW_BODY` for both DEAD and UNCONSCIOUS bodies (see body_factor decay for UNCONSCIOUS). Downstream systems that need to distinguish a sleeping colleague from a dead one (Dialogue: "Henri? wake up!" vs "Henri's dead!") branch on the observed body's `current_alert_state` â€” explicit forward-dep contract for Dialogue & Subtitles. This is the "guard trips over his buddy" comedy/tension beat. Because incapacitated guards (DEAD or UNCONSCIOUS) have `VisionCone.monitoring = false` + `HearingPoller` disabled, they do not create false-positive SAW_BODY chains by "seeing each other"; only live guards perceive.

**UNCONSCIOUS wake-up** (NEW 2026-04-22 resolves game-designer B-1 â€” user-approved mechanical differentiator for UNCONSCIOUS vs DEAD).

A guard entering `AlertState.UNCONSCIOUS` (via `MELEE_NONLETHAL` takedown or non-lethal damage at 0 HP per `Combat.is_lethal_damage_type() == false`) starts a wake-up countdown:

- `_wake_remaining_sec: float` â€” initialised to `WAKE_UP_SEC` (default 45.0 s, safe range 30â€“60 s â€” new Tuning Knob) at UNCONSCIOUS entry.
- Decrements by `خ”t` every physics frame regardless of perception state (perception is disabled, but the guard still receives `_physics_process` on its root `CharacterBody3D` for the timer).
- When `_wake_remaining_sec <= 0`: guard transitions **UNCONSCIOUS â†’ SUSPICIOUS** with `cause = SAW_PLAYER` and `LKP = Eve's last-known global position at UNCONSCIOUS entry` (captured on entry to UNCONSCIOUS and stored in `_wake_known_player_position: Vector3`). Signals emitted in this order within the same physics frame: `alert_state_changed(self, UNCONSCIOUS, SUSPICIOUS, MAJOR)` + `actor_became_alerted(self, SAW_PLAYER, _wake_known_player_position, MAJOR)`. Propagation applies per F.4 (fresh cause, not ALERTED_BY_OTHER).

**Wake re-entry cleanup** (applied synchronously within the wake-tick, in this order):

1. `current_alert_state = AlertState.SUSPICIOUS`.
2. Play `chloroformed_rising` animation blend (1.0 s pose-out transition from `chloroformed_slump` to upright `suspicious_stand`). Animator spec: 0.0â€“0.5 s guard sits up groggily; 0.5â€“1.0 s stands to SUSPICIOUS pose with weapon still holstered, head turning toward `_wake_known_player_position`.
3. Re-enable perception: `VisionCone.monitoring = true`; `HearingPoller.set_physics_process(true)`; reset `_last_handled_event_id` to invalid; reset HearingPoller tick counter with `get_instance_id() % 6` stagger (same as initial spawn).
4. Group unification: remove from `dead_guard` group, add to `alive_guard` group.
5. Accumulators primed: `_sight_accumulator = T_SUSPICIOUS + 0.01` (force SUSPICIOUS entry state to persist past the current frame even without current stimulus) ; `_sound_accumulator = 0.0`. The primed sight accumulator is sourced from `_wake_known_player_position` as a phantom stimulus â€” guard is SUSPICIOUS because they *remember* being attacked, not because they currently see/hear Eve. Normal F.3 decay applies from the next frame; if no fresh stimulus arrives, SUSPICIOUS â†’ UNAWARE timeout (4 s) lets the guard return to patrol. This is the MVP approximation of "angry waking" â€” a playtest-tunable feel.
6. Perception cache `_perception_cache.initialized = false` â†’ will be re-populated on the next F.1 tick.
7. Emit `Events.guard_woke_up(self)` project-level signal (Audio subscribes for wake music cue + ambient breathing termination; Mission Scripting for objective triggers like "Eve killed / woke all guards"). **This signal addition must be reflected in ADR-0002 amendment** (pre-impl gate).

**body_factor decay during wake-up window** (NEW 2026-04-22 resolves game-designer B-1 + audio-director R-3 concerns). While a guard is UNCONSCIOUS, the F.1 `body_factor` applied when OTHER guards sight this corpse decays linearly from 2.0أ— (at UNCONSCIOUS entry) to 1.0أ— (at wake moment) over `WAKE_UP_SEC`. The formula for an observer sighting an UNCONSCIOUS guard:

```
body_factor = 1.0 + clamp(_wake_remaining_sec / WAKE_UP_SEC, 0.0, 1.0)
```

At entry (`_wake_remaining_sec == 45`): body_factor = 2.0 (fresh body is maximally alarming). At wake (`_wake_remaining_sec == 0`): body_factor = 1.0 (matches a standing live Eve). **DEAD guards retain body_factor = 2.0 indefinitely** (no decay â€” a corpse remains alarming). This gives UNCONSCIOUS a distinct perception footprint: an early discovery by another guard is urgent; a late discovery is still a cue but not alarm-level. Combined with the fact that UNCONSCIOUS guards wake and resume play, the non-lethal choice now has real mechanical consequence vs DEAD.

**Transitional UNCONSCIOUS â†’ DEAD interaction with wake-up.** Lethal damage on an UNCONSCIOUS guard (E.20) cancels the wake-up timer: the guard transitions to DEAD, `_wake_remaining_sec` is discarded, body_factor locks at 2.0أ—. The wake-up timer is never re-armed by any path.

**MVP scope constraint**: wake-up from save/reload. Saved UNCONSCIOUS guards restore with their `_wake_remaining_sec` intact (serialised field â€” see آ§Death and save-state update below). A guard that was UNCONSCIOUS with `_wake_remaining_sec = 10 s` at save time will restore with 10 s remaining and wake normally 10 s into the reloaded session. **Exception**: if the player reloads a save that was made BEFORE the guard was chloroformed (canonical save-slot semantics), the guard restores to its pre-UNCONSCIOUS live state (Save/Load owns this â€” the UNCONSCIOUS entry is a post-save event that is not in the save). Documented as expected behaviour; not a Stealth AI responsibility.

**Propagation (alert-spreading).** When any guard transitions from UNAWARE â†’ SUSPICIOUS or higher **and the cause is NOT `ALERTED_BY_OTHER` or `CURIOSITY_BAIT`**, `Events.actor_became_alerted(self, cause, source_position, severity)` fires (severity per the rule above). Other live guards within `ALERT_PROPAGATION_RADIUS_M` receive this signal and bump their own sound accumulator by a fixed amount (see F.4), partially alerting nearby patrol groups. This models "the guard called out and his friend across the hall heard him." The propagation is **one-hop only** â€” a guard whose transition is caused by `ALERTED_BY_OTHER` does NOT re-emit `actor_became_alerted` (the transition row in the table above suppresses emission when `cause == ALERTED_BY_OTHER`). Formal invariant: the propagation graph is a tree of depth 1, rooted at the originally-stimulated guard.

**Death and save-state.** Removed-from-play guards (DEAD or UNCONSCIOUS) are serialised as `{position, rotation, alert_state, takedown_type, terminal_cause, approach_vector, wake_remaining_sec, wake_known_player_position}` per ADR-0003, where:

- `alert_state` âˆˆ {`DEAD`, `UNCONSCIOUS`} â€” the terminal state at save time.
- `takedown_type` âˆˆ {`MELEE_NONLETHAL`, `STEALTH_BLADE`, null} â€” null when the terminal state came from a damage path rather than a takedown (e.g., lethal gunfight kill, dart-damage KO from `receive_damage` without a `receive_takedown` preceding it).
- `terminal_cause` âˆˆ {`TAKEDOWN_NONLETHAL`, `TAKEDOWN_BLADE`, `DAMAGE_LETHAL`, `DAMAGE_NONLETHAL`, `TRANSITIONAL_UNCONSCIOUS_TO_DEAD`} â€” sufficient to reconstruct the correct slump pose, outline tier, and Audio routing on restore.
- `approach_vector: Vector2` â€” unit vector (XZ plane) captured at terminal-entry, used by the body-drop animator to face the guard AWAY from Eve's incoming direction (آ§V.4 Combat). For the `TRANSITIONAL_UNCONSCIOUS_TO_DEAD` cause, this field preserves the ORIGINAL takedown/KO approach, not the follow-up lethal-shot source direction.
- `wake_remaining_sec: float` (NEW 2026-04-22) â€” remaining wake-up countdown for UNCONSCIOUS guards. Ignored (value undefined) for DEAD guards. Range [0.0, WAKE_UP_SEC].
- `wake_known_player_position: Vector3` (NEW 2026-04-22) â€” Eve's global position at the moment UNCONSCIOUS was entered, used as LKP when the guard wakes. Ignored for DEAD guards.

Patrol route and suspicion state are NOT serialised for removed-from-play guards â€” they don't patrol, and their perception is disabled. Live guards serialise `{position, rotation, alert_state, patrol_index, sight_accumulator, sound_accumulator, last_known_position, search_timeout_remaining, combat_lost_target_remaining, _lkp_has_sight_confirm}`. Fields NOT serialised (regenerated on load): `_last_handled_event_id` (idempotent-read dedupe key is per-physics-frame latch; section-reload always fresh), any cached raycast results, HearingPoller tick counter (re-initialised with the `get_instance_id() % 6` stagger), `_perception_cache` (re-initialised with `initialized = false` â€” rebuilt on first F.1 tick).

**Restore behaviour.** Live guards restore serialised values then apply the E.11 implicit-decay rule (`_sight -= COMBAT_SEC_ON_RELOAD أ— SIGHT_DECAY[alert_state]`, same for `_sound`). Removed-from-play guards restore with `VisionCone.monitoring = false` + `HearingPoller.set_physics_process(false)` + group `dead_guard` + `NavigationAgent3D.target_position = global_position` + pose selected by `terminal_cause` (`chloroformed_slump` for UNCONSCIOUS, `dead_slump` for DEAD). **UNCONSCIOUS restore now resumes wake-up countdown** (revised 2026-04-22 â€” wake-up authored at MVP): `_wake_remaining_sec` and `_wake_known_player_position` restored from save; `_physics_process` kept active on the root CharacterBody3D so the countdown continues. If the save was made with `_wake_remaining_sec = 10 s`, the guard wakes 10 s into the reloaded session per the UNCONSCIOUS â†’ SUSPICIOUS transition rule. The Transitional UNCONSCIOUS â†’ DEAD edge fires only via live lethal damage on an already-loaded UNCONSCIOUS guard, not as part of restore.

**Perception ownership boundary.** PlayerCharacter publishes **source** (origin, radius, type). Stealth AI owns **propagation** (occlusion via raycast, elevation attenuation â€” NOLF1 precedent: noise does not penetrate floors; cross-floor audibility is zero â€” surface absorption modifier per ground material read from the same `surface_tag` metadata that FootstepComponent reads, distance falloff). This boundary is documented per-dependency below.

## Formulas

**Variables used throughout:**
- `خ”t` â€” physics frame delta (s, nominal 1/60 at 60 Hz)
- `خ”t_clamped = min(خ”t, 1.0 / 30.0)` â€” hitch guard, mirroring PC's pattern
- `d(a, b)` â€” Euclidean distance between two `Vector3` positions (m)
- `occlusion(a, b)` â€” returns 1.0 if unobstructed; 0.0 if fully blocked; per-obstacle partial values in (0, 1) via raycast result
- `floor_delta(a, b)` â€” absolute difference in floor-level between two positions (whole integers; NOLF1 rule: noise does not penetrate floors)

### F.1 Sight fill rate

Runs per vision-cone physics frame while Eve's `CharacterBody3D` is inside the guard's `VisionCone` Area3D AND a LOS raycast from the guard's eye position to Eve's head position succeeds.

```
sight_fill_rate = BASE_SIGHT_RATE أ— range_factor أ— silhouette_factor أ— movement_factor أ— state_multiplier أ— body_factor
_sight_accumulator += sight_fill_rate أ— خ”t_clamped
```

**Variables:**

| Variable | Symbol | Type | Range | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `BASE_SIGHT_RATE` | `B_s` | float | 1.0 (tuning knob) | Baseline full-accumulator-fill rate per second at range 0 with standing silhouette on a walking Eve in UNAWARE |
| `range_factor` | `r` | float | [0.0, 1.0] | `1.0 - clamp(d(guard.eye, eve.head) / VISION_MAX_RANGE_M, 0.0, 1.0)` â€” linear falloff from guard to `VISION_MAX_RANGE_M` (default 18 m) |
| `silhouette_factor` | `h` | float | [0.5, 1.0] | `clamp(eve.get_silhouette_height() / 1.7, 0.5, 1.0)` â€” floor 0.5 matches the minimum of Eve's standing (1.7 m) and crouching (1.1 m) silhouettes. Below-waist silhouette (i.e. fully obscured by cover) is handled by LOS failing, not by this factor. Post-MVP note: if a prone posture is added, silhouette_factor saturates at 0.5 (identical to crouch) â€” any additional prone-stealth benefit must come from LOS geometry, not this formula |
| `movement_factor` | `m` | float | [0.0, 1.5] | State-keyed: **DEAD (Eve) = 0.0** (dead Eve produces no posthumous sight fill â€” see E.10), **DEAD_TARGET (dead guard corpse) = 0.3** (corpse is not moving but body_factor 2أ— carries the escalation â€” NEW 2026-04-22 resolves systems-designer R-1), IDLE/Walk-still/Crouch-still = 0.3, Walk = 1.0, Crouch = 0.5, Sprint = 1.5, Jump/Fall = 0.8 |
| `state_multiplier` | `s` | float | {1.0, 1.5, 1.5, 2.0} | Guard's own alert state: UNAWARE = 1.0, SUSPICIOUS = 1.5, SEARCHING = 1.5, COMBAT = 2.0 |
| `body_factor` | `b` | float | {1.0, 2.0} | 1.0 when target is alive Eve (in `player` group); **2.0 when target is a dead guard (in `dead_guard` group)** â€” "seeing a corpse is faster-escalating than seeing a moving Eve." Dead-body sight always uses `AlertCause.SAW_BODY` |

**Output range:** 0.0 (Eve DEAD / at max range with minimum silhouette + still) to **6.0/sec** (dead body at close range with Combat-state guard: `1.0 أ— 1.0 أ— 1.0 أ— 1.5 أ— 2.0 أ— 2.0`). At 60 Hz, `_sight_accumulator` can rise from 0 â†’ 1 in (1/3.0) â‰ˆ **0.33 s** under worst-case alive-Eve input (`1.0 أ— 1.0 أ— 1.0 أ— 1.5 أ— 2.0 أ— 1.0 = 3.0/sec`); half that time (~0.17 s) under worst-case dead-body input. **Note on body-vs-Eve distinction**: `movement_factor` is a property of the target. When target is a dead guard (2أ— body_factor), the corpse itself is not moving â€” use the new `DEAD_TARGET` entry (`movement_factor = 0.3`, see variable table) rather than Eve's DEAD value (0.0). This keeps the SAW_BODY 2أ—-fill mechanic functional. For UNCONSCIOUS bodies (pre-wake), see آ§Detailed Rules wake-up section: `body_factor` decays 2.0أ— â†’ 1.0أ— linearly over the wake-up window; `movement_factor` uses DEAD_TARGET until wake (chloroformed_rising animation â†’ SUSPICIOUS transition).

**Example** (crouching Eve at 6 m from a SUSPICIOUS guard's eye):
- `range_factor = 1.0 - (6/18) = 0.667`
- `silhouette_factor = clamp(1.1/1.7, 0.5, 1.0) = 0.647`
- `movement_factor = 0.5` (Crouch)
- `state_multiplier = 1.5` (SUSPICIOUS)
- `sight_fill_rate = 1.0 أ— 0.667 أ— 0.647 أ— 0.5 أ— 1.5 = 0.324` per second
- Accumulator fills 0 â†’ 1 in ~3.1 s â€” Eve has 3 seconds to break LOS before guard escalates (SEARCHING threshold) or sees positively (COMBAT threshold).

**LOS gate:** fill rate is 0 if the LOS raycast (from guard's eye position to Eve's head position) hits anything on `MASK_AI_VISION_OCCLUDERS`. Eve's silhouette height is used so a crouching Eve behind a desk is occluded even if her head would otherwise be in cone.

**Decay** (when LOS breaks OR Eve leaves vision cone):
```

```
Where `SIGHT_DECAY_RATE` is state-keyed per F.3. Never below 0.

**Implementation note â€” raycast caching.** F.1 (sight LOS) and F.2a (occlusion factor) both raycast the pair `guard â†’ Eve` on mask `MASK_AI_VISION_OCCLUDERS`. On any physics frame where both are invoked, the implementation MUST cache the F.1 result and reuse it for F.2a (and vice-versa) â€” the query is identical. Reduces 2 raycasts per guard per active frame to 1. With 12 guards أ— 60 Hz this halves raycast budget from 1440/sec worst-case to 720/sec. Cache lifetime is single physics frame; stale-check via frame counter.

### F.2 Sound fill rate

Runs on the guard's `HearingPoller` at 10 Hz (every 6 physics frames at 60 Hz). Two sub-cases:

#### F.2a Continuous locomotion sound (polled 10 Hz)

```
noise_at_source = player.get_noise_level()  # returns 0 if Eve is still/dead

audibility = max(0.0, effective_radius - d(guard, eve.global_transform.origin))

```

**Variables:**

| Variable | Symbol | Type | Range | Description |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `noise_at_source` | `nâ‚€` | float | [0.0, 12.0] m | From PC's `get_noise_level()` (ship-multiplier 1.0 â€” Walk 5, Sprint 12, Crouch 3, 0 when still/dead) |
| `occlusion_factor` | `o` | float | [0.0, 1.0] | From a raycast between guard and Eve on `MASK_AI_VISION_OCCLUDERS` â€” 1.0 unobstructed; 0.25 through one wall (3 dB attenuation equivalent); 0.0 through two walls |
| `elevation_factor` | `e` | float | {1.0, 0.0} | `1.0` if `floor_delta(guard, eve) == 0`; **`0.0` otherwise** (NOLF1 precedent: noise does not penetrate floors). Cross-floor audibility is zero |
| `surface_factor` | `f` | float | [0.5, 1.5] | Read from Eve's ground-surface `surface_tag` (same meta FootstepComponent reads): `marble` = 1.2, `tile` = 1.1, `wood_stage` = 1.3, `carpet` = 0.5, `metal_grate` = 1.5, `gravel` = 0.9, `default` = 1.0. Wood_stage amplifies; carpet attenuates |

| `AUDIBILITY_DIVISOR` | `D` | float | 10.0 (knob) | Normaliser: at 10 m audibility excess AND state_multiplier=1, fill rate = 1.0 / s (accumulator reaches T_SUSPICIOUS â‰ˆ 0.3 in ~0.3 s) |
| `state_multiplier` | `s` | float | {1.0, 1.5, 1.5, 2.0} | Same as F.1 â€” guard alerts faster when already on edge |

**Output range:** 0.0 (Eve inaudible) to ~4.8 per second (Eve sprinting through a metal grate 2 m from a combat guard). Typical Walk-within-range gives 0.3â€“0.6 per second.

**Example** (Eve walking on carpet 4 m from an UNAWARE guard, unobstructed, same floor):
- `nâ‚€ = 5.0` (Walk); `o = 1.0`, `e = 1.0`, `f = 0.5` (carpet)
- `r_eff = 5.0 أ— 1.0 أ— 1.0 أ— 0.5 = 2.5 m`
- `audibility = max(0, 2.5 - 4.0) = 0` â€” inaudible. Walk on carpet at 4 m is safe.

**Example** (Eve sprinting on marble 8 m from the same guard, unobstructed, same floor):
- `nâ‚€ = 12.0` (Sprint), `f = 1.2` (marble)
- `r_eff = 12.0 أ— 1.0 أ— 1.0 أ— 1.2 = 14.4 m`
- `audibility = max(0, 14.4 - 8.0) = 6.4 m`
- `sound_fill_rate = 6.4 / 10.0 أ— 1.0 = 0.64 per second`
- Per 10 Hz tick: accumulator gains 0.064. To reach `T_SUSPICIOUS` (default 0.3) takes 5 ticks = 0.5 s.

#### F.2b Discrete spike sound (polled every frame; spikes can expire in 0.15 s window)

```
event = player.get_noise_event()

    # Idempotent-read per PC F.4: every guard in the 0.15 s window sees this same event

    audibility = max(0.0, effective_radius - d(guard, event.origin))  # NOTE: event.origin, not player position
    sound_fill_rate = audibility / AUDIBILITY_DIVISOR أ— state_multiplier أ— EVENT_WEIGHT[event.type]
    # Spike cap: a single spike event may raise the accumulator to AT MOST (T_SEARCHING - 0.01).
    # This preserves graduated-suspicion (Pillar 3): no single acoustic SPIKE event â€” not even the loudest
    # hard landing on metal grate â€” can put a guard directly into COMBAT. Combat from a single spike
    # requires a second confirmation (continuous sight, another spike, or sustained sound).
    # NOTE: the F.2a continuous channel is NOT capped; sustained audible locomotion (e.g., Eve
    # sprinting on metal_grate adjacent to a guard) can saturate `_sound_accumulator` to 1.0 and
    # therefore cross T_COMBAT via the combined-score rule. This is the intended "close-adjacent
    # sustained exposure" edge â€” rare in practice, reachable by design.
    spike_ceiling = T_SEARCHING - 0.01  # = 0.59 at default tuning
    _sound_accumulator = min(max(_sound_accumulator, _sound_accumulator + sound_fill_rate), spike_ceiling) if _sound_accumulator < spike_ceiling else _sound_accumulator
    # Semantic: if accumulator already â‰¥ spike_ceiling (e.g. sustained sound has already pushed it
    # past 0.59), spikes cannot reduce it (take max). If below ceiling, spike may push up to ceiling
    # but not above. The continuous channel F.2a + the sight channel F.1 remain free to push higher.
    _sound_accumulator = clamp(_sound_accumulator, 0.0, 1.0)  # defence-in-depth hard cap
    _last_handled_event_id = event_identity(event)  # local dedupe across polls within same latch window
    _last_known_position = event.origin  # update LKP for investigate (sight LKPs have priority â€” see E.3)
```

**Per ADR-0002 IG4**: `event` is a reference to a `NoiseEvent` RefCounted/Resource owned by the Player Character. `Vector3` and scalar fields read from it are value-copied by GDScript assignment (so `var origin = event.origin` is safe on its own), BUT the guard MUST NOT retain the `NoiseEvent` reference across physics frames â€” the PC may reuse the object and mutate its fields. Always copy the scalar fields you need (`type`, `radius_m`, `origin`) into guard-local variables in the same frame you read them; never store `event` itself. `event_identity(event)` is the tuple `(event.type, event.origin)` â€” stable within the latch window â€” used as a local dedupe key so the same spike doesn't fire the one-shot add twice if the guard polls twice during the 9-frame latch.

**EVENT_WEIGHT table:**

| Event type | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `JUMP_TAKEOFF` (4 m) | 0.5 | Minor â€” a footfall-level blip |
| `LANDING_SOFT` (5 m) | 0.7 | Audible landing cue |
| `LANDING_HARD` (8â€“16 m scaled) | 1.5 | Loud â€” contains explicit intent signal |
| `BAIT_SOURCE` (8 m) | 1.0 | NEW 2026-04-24 per Inventory Coord item #7. Sprint-tier weight bracket (~1.0) so sustained ticking drives a SUSPICIOUS investigation without reaching COMBAT via a single tick. | `FOOTSTEP_*` | â€” | These are continuous-channel values already counted in F.2a; NOT treated as spike events. Present in the enum for completeness but the latch only fires for the jump/landing subset per PC F.4 |

**Example** (Eve hard-lands from a 3 m drop near a guard at 5 m distance, same floor, marble surface):
- `|v.y|` at landing â‰ˆ `sqrt(2 أ— 12 أ— 3) â‰ˆ 7.75 m/s`
- `v_land_hard = 6 m/s`
- Ratio = 7.75 / 6 = 1.29; clamped 1.0â€“2.0 â†’ 1.29
- PC emits `NoiseEvent{type=LANDING_HARD, radius_m=8 أ— 1.29 = 10.33, origin=landing}`
- Guard sees: `effective_radius = 10.33 أ— 1.0 أ— 1.0 أ— 1.2 = 12.4 m`; `audibility = 12.4 - 5 = 7.4 m`
- `sound_fill_rate = 7.4 / 10 أ— 1.0 أ— 1.5 = 1.11` (one-shot, uncapped)
- **After spike cap applied** (ceiling = `T_SEARCHING - 0.01 = 0.59`): accumulator rises from 0.0 to 0.59, NOT to 1.11. Guard transitions UNAWARE â†’ SEARCHING (crosses `T_SEARCHING = 0.6`? No â€” 0.59 < 0.6, so guard transitions to SUSPICIOUS, not SEARCHING, in this isolated example. With the combined-score weighting, if `_sight > 0` this pushes over T_SEARCHING â€” a sight+sound confirmation reaches SEARCHING organically). **UNAWARE â†’ COMBAT from a SINGLE hard landing is impossible by the spike cap WHEN `_sight == 0`** (corrected 2026-04-22 per systems-designer B-2). **When prior sight has been accumulating (`_sight â‰¥ ~0.7`)** a post-spike combined score can cross `T_COMBAT`: e.g., `_sight = 0.7` + spike-capped `_sound = 0.59` â†’ `combined = 0.7 + 0.5 أ— 0.59 = 0.995 â‰¥ 0.95` â†’ COMBAT. Pillar 3 preserved for the ZERO-PRIOR-SIGHT case (the common spike-alone case from a hidden position). (Sustained continuous noise via F.2a remains uncapped â€” see spike-cap commentary above.) ### F.3 Accumulator decay

Runs per physics frame regardless of stimulus presence:

```
if sight_not_refreshed_this_frame:
    _sight_accumulator = max(0.0, _sight_accumulator - SIGHT_DECAY[state] أ— خ”t_clamped)
if sound_not_refreshed_this_poll:
    _sound_accumulator = max(0.0, _sound_accumulator - SOUND_DECAY[state] أ— خ”t_clamped)
```

**Decay rate table** (units: per second, tuning knobs):

| Guard state | SIGHT_DECAY | SOUND_DECAY |
|---|---|---|
| UNAWARE | 0.5 | 0.4 |
| SUSPICIOUS | 0.3 | 0.25 |
| SEARCHING | 0.15 | 0.12 |
| COMBAT | 0.05 | 0.05 |

Rationale: Combat-state guards retain alarm far longer (they've committed). UNAWARE decays fastest (false alarms evaporate).

**Output range:** 0.0 (floor) to current-accumulator-value (starting point). Decay never goes negative.

**Example** (guard loses sight at `_sight = 0.8` in SEARCHING state):
- Decay = 0.15 per second
- Time to reach T_DECAY_UNAWARE (0.1): (0.8 - 0.1) / 0.15 = 4.67 s
- Guard must then wait SEARCH_TIMEOUT_SEC additionally before de-escalating to SUSPICIOUS.

### F.4 Alert-propagation (guard-to-guard)

When any guard fires `Events.actor_became_alerted(self, cause, source_position, severity)`, every live guard within `ALERT_PROPAGATION_RADIUS_M` receives an accumulator bump (propagation does NOT fire when `cause == ALERTED_BY_OTHER`, `cause == CURIOSITY_BAIT`, or `cause == SCRIPTED` â€” see one-hop rule and آ§Interactions Mission Scripting row restriction 3. Scripted beats target specific guards by design; chain-alerting during a scripted moment is the level designer's responsibility to express explicitly):

```
if d(self, other_guard) <= ALERT_PROPAGATION_RADIUS_M and self != other_guard and other_guard.alert_state not in [AlertState.DEAD, AlertState.UNCONSCIOUS]:
    bump = PROPAGATION_BUMP أ— floor_delta_factor(self, other_guard)
    other_guard._sound_accumulator = clamp(other_guard._sound_accumulator + bump, 0.0, 1.0)
    # The resulting transition (if any) uses cause = ALERTED_BY_OTHER; per the Severity rule
    # such a transition is always MINOR, and the transition table suppresses the re-emission
    # of actor_became_alerted. alert_state_changed DOES still fire so Audio's dominant-guard
    # aggregation stays correct.
```

**Variables:**

| Variable | Type | Range | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| `ALERT_PROPAGATION_RADIUS_M` | float | 25.0 (knob) | Guard's callout carries ~25 m in a straight line |
| `PROPAGATION_BUMP` | float | 0.4 (knob) | Brings UNAWARE guard to SUSPICIOUS threshold on one hop (T_SUSPICIOUS = 0.3); combined with existing `_sight` a bumped guard may reach SEARCHING â€” this is intended "the friend's shout tips the second guard over an edge that was already building" |
| `floor_delta_factor` | float | {1.0, 0.0} | 1.0 if same floor; 0.0 otherwise (NOLF1 rule) |

**One-hop only (formal invariant).** A guard whose current transition `cause == ALERTED_BY_OTHER` does NOT re-emit `actor_became_alerted` (the transition table suppresses this). Only first-hand stimulus (sight, sound, SAW_BODY, HEARD_GUNFIRE) fires propagation. `CURIOSITY_BAIT` and `SCRIPTED` causes also do NOT propagate (excluded from F.4 above). Propagation graph is a tree of depth 1, rooted at the originally-stimulated guard. `alert_state_changed` still fires on the bumped guard's transition (so Audio's music-state dict remains consistent) â€” but with severity MINOR, so no brass-punch stinger plays.

**Removed-from-play guards cannot be propagation sources (formal invariant, NEW 2026-04-22 resolves ai-programmer B-6).** A guard in `AlertState.UNCONSCIOUS` or `AlertState.DEAD` shall never emit `actor_became_alerted`. This is implicitly guaranteed by the terminal-entry cleanup sequence (VisionCone.monitoring = false, HearingPoller disabled, accumulators zeroed, perception processing gated by `_is_alive()` precondition), but is formally stated here so future code paths (e.g., scripted events, wake-up transitions) cannot silently violate it. Any perception-tick / signal-emission entry point must gate with `assert(current_alert_state in [UNAWARE, SUSPICIOUS, SEARCHING, COMBAT])` in debug builds. The wake-up edge (UNCONSCIOUS â†’ SUSPICIOUS, see آ§Detailed Rules wake-up section) re-enters a live state and re-enables perception BEFORE the guard can fire `actor_became_alerted` on its next tick.

**Same-state transition idempotence (consumer contract).** When propagation bumps multiple guards in the same physics frame, each emits its own `alert_state_changed(_, UNAWARE, SUSPICIOUS, MINOR)`. Audio's music state machine MUST treat a transition whose target equals the current music state as a no-op (no tween restart). Stealth AI cannot guard this â€” it publishes per-guard state changes honestly. Audio GDD آ§Edge Cases owns the dominant-guard dict idempotence rule (re-review gate flagged below).

**Tuning-coupling invariant â€” PROPAGATION_BUMP must exceed T_SUSPICIOUS.** One-hop propagation can only escalate a fresh UNAWARE guard to SUSPICIOUS if `PROPAGATION_BUMP > T_SUSPICIOUS`. Current defaults 0.4 > 0.3 âœ“. If either knob is tuned (safe ranges overlap), verify this inequality holds at `_ready()` via assertion; otherwise one-hop propagation silently fails to trigger.

**Output range:** one-shot bump of 0.4 per callout received (clamped so accumulator never exceeds 1.0). A guard receiving two callouts in quick succession can reach SEARCHING directly via the combined-score path.

### F.5 State-transition thresholds

Single source of truth for the 5 thresholds:

| Threshold | Default | Safe range | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| `T_SUSPICIOUS` | 0.3 | 0.2 â€“ 0.4 | Accumulator value above which UNAWARE â†’ SUSPICIOUS |
| `T_SEARCHING` | 0.6 | 0.5 â€“ 0.75 | Above which â†’ SEARCHING |
| `T_COMBAT` | 0.95 | 0.9 â€“ 1.0 | Above which â†’ COMBAT (sight-only in practice; sound alone should not cross this) |
| `T_DECAY_UNAWARE` | 0.1 | 0.05 â€“ 0.2 | `combined` must fall below this for SUSPICIOUS â†’ UNAWARE de-escalate (with timeout) |
| `T_DECAY_SEARCHING` | 0.35 | 0.25 â€“ 0.45 | Resting-point set on SEARCHING â†’ SUSPICIOUS de-escalate (guard remains edgy briefly) |

Escalation uses `combined >= threshold` where `combined = max(_sight, _sound) + 0.5 أ— min(_sight, _sound)`. De-escalation uses `combined < threshold` AND the state's timeout.

**Output range:** each threshold is a scalar in [0.0, 1.0]; each accumulator is clamped to `[0.0, 1.0]` at every add-step (F.2a/F.2b/F.4) and floored at 0.0 by decay (F.3). `combined` has range `[0.0, 1.5]` (when both accumulators saturate at 1.0: `combined = 1.0 + 0.5 أ— 1.0 = 1.5`) â€” this intentional over-1.0 ceiling gives the combined channel headroom above T_COMBAT so cross-channel confirmations feel decisive.

## Edge Cases

- **E.1 Eve crouching on carpet behind cover with broken LOS**: `_sight_accumulator = 0` (no LOS), `_sound_accumulator` â†’ 0 (carpet attenuation أ— no visible = inaudible at normal Walk). Guard remains UNAWARE. Correct Pillar 3 reward for patience.
- **E.2 Simultaneous sight + sound on same frame**: sight fills `_sight`, sound fills `_sound` independently. Escalation uses the `combined` score. If `combined` crosses a threshold, one state transition fires; AlertCause is tie-broken by which accumulator contributed the larger term (sight if `_sight >= _sound`, otherwise sound).
- **E.3 LKP overwrite race**: Two stimuli in the same physics frame update LKP. **Sight LKPs always take priority over sound LKPs** (sight localisation is more reliable than sound). A flag `_lkp_has_sight_confirm: bool` is set true on sight-sourced writes; sound writes are ignored while it remains true for the current SEARCHING dwell. The flag clears on state transition out of SEARCHING or on successful sweep completion. Two sight writes or two sound writes in the same frame: second write wins.
- **E.4 Guard in SEARCHING arrives at LKP that is now stale** (Eve has moved 8 m away): guard completes sweep, finds nothing, de-escalates per F.3 normal path. Eve is safe at her new position UNLESS her continuous-locomotion noise refills the sound channel during the sweep. This is the "I hear the guard searching and I'm holding perfectly still" tension beat.
- **E.5 Guard spawned mid-alert**: on section load from save, restored accumulators drive the correct entry state. If restored to COMBAT but Eve isn't in the scene yet, guard enters SEARCHING (no sight target) after 1 physics frame and proceeds from LKP.
- **E.6 Takedown attempted on SEARCHING/COMBAT guard**: the attempt returns false; `receive_takedown` does NOT transition state; instead fires `actor_became_alerted(self, SAW_PLAYER, attacker.global_transform.origin, MAJOR)` and triggers propagation (failed takedown is a real first-hand detection). Emits one "you tried" vocal.
- **E.7 Two guards simultaneously discover the same body**: each guard independently crosses the `SAW_BODY` sight threshold via the combined-score rule, transitions to SEARCHING, fires `actor_became_alerted(self, SAW_BODY, body.position, MAJOR)`. Propagation bumps are still applied to both guards by each other â€” they're already at SEARCHING so the bump is harmless (accumulators already clamped near 1.0). Each guard tracks its own alert state independently.

- **E.22 Re-takedown attempt on UNCONSCIOUS or DEAD guard** (NEW 2026-04-22 â€” OQ-CD-1): Eve approaches an already-UNCONSCIOUS or DEAD guard from the rear arc and presses the Takedown input. `takedown_prompt_active(eve)` returns `false` (eligibility requires `current_alert_state in {UNAWARE, SUSPICIOUS}`), so Combat's Takedown-input gate (CR-10 revised) suppresses the prompt and drops the input silently â€” no animation, no audio, no SAI call. - **E.23 Approach-vector degenerate cases** (NEW 2026-04-22 â€” OQ-CD-1 / آ§V.4 Combat): (a) Attacker at exactly the same XZ position as the guard (vertical alignment â€” e.g., Eve standing on the guard's head via level exploit): `(attacker.origin - self.origin).with_y(0)` has magnitude 0 and `normalized()` returns the zero vector.
