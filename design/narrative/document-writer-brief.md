## 1. Voice Exemplars (per section, 5 sections)

A voice exemplar is one paragraph written in the section's tonal register, intended as a tuning fork for the writer. It is NOT a draft document — it is a register reference.

### Plaza — Surface Legitimacy (civilian Tower paper; PHANTOM hidden behind the venue)

> *"AVIS AUX VISITEURS. La présente registration est requise pour l'accès aux niveaux supérieurs après dix-neuf heures, conformément au Règlement du Bureau du Tourisme de Paris (article 14, sous-article c). Les visiteurs munis d'une carte annuelle sont priés de la présenter au comptoir d'enregistrement. La direction décline toute responsabilité en cas de perte d'effets personnels au-delà du présent comptoir."*

The register is municipal, polite, slightly bureaucratic. Tower is a tourist venue; it disclaims liability the way every venue does. PHANTOM is not yet visible in the paper.

### Lower Scaffolds — Workplace Revealed (PHANTOM's administrative layer surfaces)

> *"Roster — Équipe B — semaine du 14 au 20 avril. Henri prend la garde du matin (06:00–14:00). Marc relève à 14:00 jusqu'à 22:00. Le poste de nuit reste à pourvoir; voir M. Aubry. Note: la bouilloire de la salle de pause est encore en panne. J'ai écrit au service de maintenance pour la troisième fois. CC: M. Aubry, Mme Lacombe. — R."*

Workplace paper. The duty roster names roles ("poste de nuit") that don't exist in legitimate Tower operations — PHANTOM is staffing through the night. The kettle complaint is filed three times. The clerk has correspondence with a Mme Lacombe who does not work for the Tower.

### Restaurant — Social Cover as Paperwork (operation requires the restaurant to function)

> *"Mémo Direction — Service du soir 14 avril. Réservation pour M. Vogel, Salle Privée 3, 20h00, table de quatre couverts. Restrictions alimentaires (impératif): pas de sulfites — réf. allergie médicalement documentée, dossier au coffre. Cave: pas de Sancerre 1962 (sulfites élevés). Recommander Bordeaux 1959. Le maître d'hôtel à confirmer la composition du dessert avec le chef pâtissier avant 18h00. — Mme L."*

Restaurant administration. Vogel is named; his dietary restriction is sulfite allergy. Comedy is at zero distance from the bioweapon plot — the dietary memo and the bioweapon stabilizer share a chemical class. The clerk doesn't know.

### Upper Structure — Command Paperwork (telexes, technical specs, distribution-list memos)

> *"PHANTOM PARIS — DIRECTIVE OPÉRATIONNELLE 14/04/65 — DISTRIBUTION RESTREINTE — Équipement Section 4 (Niveau Antenne): vérification de calibration achevée 13/04 à 22h30 par technicien désigné. Tous paramètres dans les tolérances opérationnelles. Tableau de bord transmis à la Direction Scientifique pour archivage. CC: M. Vogel, M. Rénier (Direction). RÉF: PT-65-114. INITIALES: A.B. / J.M. / R.L."*

Command-directive register. Triple-initialled (the more formal, the more important). Vogel is now a Director, not a guest with sulfite allergy. The instrument is "Section 4 equipment" — the bioweapon, never named.

### Bomb Chamber — Final Document Set (institution at the edge of its purpose)

> *"TRANSMIS PAR TÉLEX — 14 AVRIL 1965 — 23H47 — DESTINATAIRE: PARIS-CENTRAL-OPÉRATIONS — EXPÉDITEUR: TOUR-NIVEAU-ANTENNE — TEXTE: DÉTONATION CONFIRMÉE POUR 00H30 LE 15 AVRIL CONFORMÉMENT AU CALENDRIER OPÉRATIONNEL. INITIALES OPÉRATEUR R.L. AUTORISATION VOGEL CONFIRMÉE PAR DIRECTIVE PT-65-117. — FIN DE TRANSMISSION."*

All-caps teletype. Detonation is confirmed in the same operational register as the Plaza tourist-registration disclaimer — and that register-equivalence IS the comedy. The clerk is doing his job. The job is the apocalypse.

## 2. Named-Clerk Cast (PHANTOM clerks + BQA correspondents)

The "cast of clerks through paper" promise in §B Player Fantasy of the Document Collection GDD is delivered through repeating named characters. Each clerk has a tone, a role, and a documented presence across the roster. **Players never meet these characters in cutscene or dialogue — only in the paper.**

### PHANTOM clerks (6 named):

1. **Henri Aubry** — Lower Scaffolds night-shift foreman. Tone: terse, practical, complains about the kettle. Initials: **H.A.** Appears in: 2 maintenance logs (Lower), 1 attendance register (Lower), 1 personnel-dossier-author byline (Upper, on Eve's dossier).
2. **Marc R. ("R.")** — Day-shift maintenance clerk. Single-initial signature is his trademark. Writes in the first person more than anyone else. Tone: dry, slightly resentful, takes pride in his clipboard order. Appears in: 2 clipboards (Lower), 1 Personal Communication (Restaurant — to Henri, complaining about the new schedule).
3. **Mme Léa Lacombe ("Mme L.")** — Restaurant operations coordinator (PHANTOM cell's hospitality cover). Tone: professional, exact, never wastes a word. Appears in: 3 Service Documents (Restaurant — Vogel reservation memo, kitchen rota, wine ledger annotation), 1 Operational Memo (Restaurant on-path — the Vogel dietary memo, signed twice with different ink).
4. **Auguste Bréville ("A.B.")** — Comms operator, Upper Structure. Initials all telexes he transcribes. Tone: military teletype — neutral, disciplined, all-caps when transcribing operational telexes. Appears in: 2 Telex Transcripts (Upper), 1 Telex Transcript (Bomb — the detonation telex, his initials A.B. visible in the routing block alongside the operator R.L.).
5. **Jean-Pierre Mareschal ("J.M.")** — Director of Operations administration. Tone: hierarchical, formal, distributes copies aggressively (CC: lists are long). Appears in: 2 Operational Memos (Upper), 1 Technical Specification (Bomb — calibration sign-off).
6. **Renée Lefèvre ("R.L.")** — Senior comms operator, Bomb Chamber. Signs the detonation telex. Initials match Marc R.'s on first glance — deliberate red herring; players who track initials notice they're different surnames. Tone: indistinguishable from any other PHANTOM operator's; the comedy is that the apocalypse-button is pressed by someone whose clerical tone is identical to the kettle-complaint clerk's.

### BQA correspondents (3 named):

7. **Capt. Margaret Holloway, BQA** — Eve's section chief. Personal Communication only. Appears in: 1 Personal Communication (Lower — handwritten card on the back of a Tower postcard, hidden behind a maintenance pin-board, instruction to abort if "the eel is on the menu"; cryptic by necessity).
8. **Sir Reginald Underhill, BQA** — Eve's earlier mission's senior. Telex correspondence intercepted by PHANTOM. Appears in: 1 Telex Transcript (Upper — intercepted BQA cable, re-typed by PHANTOM for the Eve dossier, original date 1963).
9. **Anonymous BQA Operations Clerk** — Initials never given. Appears in: 1 Personnel Dossier letterhead (Lower — the "BQA — Paris Station" style letterhead PHANTOM uses to mock-up Eve's dossier; the joke is PHANTOM has accurate BQA letterhead because they captured BQA paper).

### Tonal hierarchy

The named cast lets the writer construct comedy via sequencing: a memo from Mme L. (Restaurant operations coordinator) can be read in tandem with a telex from A.B. (Comms operator) — the player notices the same person, Vogel, mentioned in both, with completely different formality registers, and the joke lands. **Documents that don't carry a named author/recipient are forbidden** (per Document Collection §C.7 Rule 3); this brief makes that rule operationalisable.

## 4. BQA Register Guide

The Bureau of Queen's Affairs (BQA) appears in 3 documents on the player roster (Eve's dossier in Lower; Capt. Holloway's intercepted card in Lower; Sir Reginald's intercepted telex in Upper). **Per Document Collection CR-19 / FP-DC-11, the acronym "BQA" is NEVER expanded in any document, in any locale.**

### What this means in practice

- Letterheads use `BQA — Paris Station` or `BQA Directorate — For Internal Distribution Only`. Never the full expansion.
- Personal communication uses `the Bureau` or `the Office` or `our people`. Never the full expansion.
- PHANTOM intelligence references `BQA (identity of acronym unconfirmed)`. The literal phrase "identity of acronym unconfirmed" is part of the joke — PHANTOM has a complete dossier on Eve but can't agree what the acronym stands for.

### Rationale (revised tonal framing)

Per design-review narrative-director Finding 3 (2026-04-27): the BQA-never-expanded rule is **NOT primarily Pillar-1 typographic comedy**. It is institutional voice and brand discipline. The acronym creates productive mystery and tonal consistency. PHANTOM's "identity of acronym unconfirmed" is the comedy; the suppression itself is the brand discipline.

### Locale enforcement

The CR-19 rule applies to **all locales**, not just English. A French translator cannot render BQA as "Bureau des Affaires de la Reine" — the acronym must remain BQA verbatim. The Localization Scaffold authoring guideline (per §F.5 BLOCKING coord item #3) is amended to grep for any expansion across all locale CSV files.

### Example BQA letterhead (used on Eve's dossier in Lower):

> ```
> ═══════════════════════════════════════
>   BQA — PARIS STATION
>   For Internal Distribution Only
>   Classified — Eyes Only
> ═══════════════════════════════════════
> ```

PHANTOM has accurate BQA letterhead because they captured BQA stationery. The dossier on Eve uses real BQA letterhead even though PHANTOM authored the dossier — that's the structural joke. The Eve dossier is a PHANTOM document mocked up on captured BQA paper.

## 5. Contradiction Policy + Canonical-Fact Registry

Per design-review narrative-director Finding 7 (2026-04-27): no document may contradict a fact established in any other document. The Writer maintains a canonical-fact registry; the Localization audit greps proposed translation key bodies against the registry.

### Policy rule

> **No document may contradict a fact established by another document or by the canonical-fact registry below. If a Writer needs to introduce a new fact that is not in the registry, the registry must be updated FIRST, then all related documents reviewed for consistency.**

## 7.5 Body Word-Count Ceiling (added 2026-04-27 — closes OQ-DOV-COORD-4 from `design/gdd/document-overlay-ui.md`)

**Hard ceiling**: **250 words English** for the body of any document. **No minimum**. Documents shorter than 100 words are explicitly permitted and protected — terse 30–50 word dispatches (a one-line BQA cable, a manager's standing-reservation note, a single-sentence kettle complaint) are often the strongest comedy beats in the roster, because the comedy is in *who would write this and how*, and brevity sharpens that joke.

### Why the ceiling exists

The Document Overlay UI (`design/gdd/document-overlay-ui.md` §B Player Fantasy "The Lectern Pause" + §G.3 Tuning Knobs) is built around **single-read absorption**: Eve stops walking, gives the page her full attention, reads it once, and continues. A 250-word English body at the BQA dossier-register reading speed yields ~75 seconds of attention; that is the upper bound of what the Lectern Pause fantasy can sustain before the player's posture shifts from "patient observation" to "scrolling a long document," which is a different gesture and a worse fantasy.

### Why no floor

The previous 150-word floor (now removed per game-designer 2026-04-27 design-review feedback) risked producing verbose lore documents — writers filling space to hit a floor — which would undermine **Pillar 1: Comedy Without Punchlines**. The joke must not be explained; a 30-word dispatch from a clerk who does not know what they are filing is funnier than a 200-word dispatch from a clerk who is given enough room to elaborate.

### Localization expansion budget

| Locale | Expansion factor | 250 English words → rendered words |
|---|---|---|
| English (base) | 1.0× | 250 |
| French | ~1.3× | 325 |
| German (workplace/dossier register) | up to 1.5× | 375 |
| Russian (Cyrillic) | ~1.2× | 300 |
| (CJK locales — out of MVP scope) | character-density different; treat separately | n/a |

The Document Overlay UI's `BodyScrollContainer` accommodates any of these expansions via auto-scroll (Document Overlay GDD §F.4 + §E.11). The 250-word English ceiling is the **authoring constraint**; expanded rendered word count is the natural consequence of localization, not an authoring concern.

### Workflow check (step 5)

Before drafting the body for any document beyond the Plaza tutorial set:

- Count target words for the English body. If > 250, the document MUST be split into two documents OR cut to ≤ 250.
- If the document is on-path narrative-critical and cannot be cut without losing required information, escalate to Narrative Director for a creative-director gate decision (per anchor test: would a 1965 professional reader accept this length on the page in front of her?).
- The 250-word check is a hard gate — passing it does NOT exempt the document from §C.7 tonal rules + §C.8 forbidden patterns.

### Per-document tracking

When submitting a document for review (workflow step 10), include in the submission header:

```
- Word count (English body): NNN
- Within ceiling: yes/no
- If "no", escalation status: <Narrative Director sign-off / not yet reviewed>
```

This makes the constraint operationalisable in PR review and prevents word-count drift across the 21-document roster.
