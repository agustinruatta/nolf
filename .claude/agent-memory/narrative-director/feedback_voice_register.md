The BQA register is the single governing voice standard for all narrative text surfaces (cards, captions, dossiers, documents). These constraints are validated by the dialogue-writer-brief.md VT rules and the §C text rules (TR-1 through TR-8).

**Never use "MISSION ACCOMPLISHED"** — the correct terminal status stamp is `STATUS: CLOSED`. This is non-negotiable.

**Why:** Period-bureaucratic register. The BQA files dossiers. It does not declare triumph. "MISSION ACCOMPLISHED" is modern action-game vocabulary that breaks both Pillar 1 (it is a punchline, not a witty silence) and Pillar 5 (it is not 1965 intelligence-service language).

**How to apply:** Any card, caption, or dossier surface that signals mission completion uses `STATUS: CLOSED`. If generating model copy, never draft "MISSION ACCOMPLISHED," "SUCCESS," "COMPLETE," or second-person praise.

---

**Eve does not speak in any authored content except scripted BQA radio**

**Why:** VT-1 from dialogue-writer-brief.md. Eve's silence is load-bearing — the `[STERLING.]` telex register lands as typographic wit only against an otherwise-silent Eve. Cards, cinematics, and objective confirmations are written in the BQA bureaucratic voice, never in Eve's voice.

**How to apply:** Card copy uses passive bureaucratic construction. Cinematics have no Eve VO except the one authorized radio line in the rappel cinematic (`[HANDLER]: Sterling. Clock is running.` — this is the HANDLER speaking, not Eve). Eve never narrates her own actions.

---

**No exclamation marks anywhere on cards or in captions**

**Why:** TR-5. The typewritten dossier register does not exclaim. The 1960s teleprinter register does not exclaim. An exclamation mark would break both Pillar 1 (a different kind of punchline — too obvious) and Pillar 5 (anachronistic expressiveness).

**How to apply:** Review every authored card line. If there is an exclamation mark, replace with a period or restructure.

---

**The "short hold" on `CHARGE: SET` card (1.0 s dismiss-gate) is deliberate comedy**

**Why:** The card fires while a demolition charge is counting down. A long hold would be ludicrous in a different, unfunny way. The brevity of the card's hold is the joke — bureaucratic acknowledgment of an explosion in progress.

**How to apply:** If adjusting tuning knobs on objective card hold times, do not equalize them to a standard duration. The Restaurant kitchen egress card's short hold is authored intent.
