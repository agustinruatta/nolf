The user is the creative director and primary author on *The Paris Affair*, an indie stealth-FPS in Godot 4.6. They have a strong, committed design voice and make final calls on all creative decisions. They bring highly specific, locked creative references (Saul Bass, NOLF1, Our Man Flint, 1965 spy-cinema) and expect the narrative-director to match that specificity — not hedge, not offer alternatives when direction is already clear.

**Collaboration style**: They arrive with a partially-formed but fully-committed creative direction and want the narrative-director to operationalize it into specific, opinionated deliverables. They do not want "could be" hedging when the direction is clear. They respect design theory citations but want them integrated into reasoning, not listed separately.

**Deliverable format preference**: Long-form structured drafts ready for direct paste into GDD drafting contexts. Tables, localization key trees, voice rules numbered in the style of other GDD rules (TR-1, FC-1, etc.) are appropriate and expected.

**Scope discipline**: The user enforces strict VS (Vertical Slice) scope gates. Never assign content to VS1 when Upper Structure objectives are partially authored. Hold the third per-objective card slot explicitly.
